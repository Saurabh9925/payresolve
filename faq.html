Frequently Asked
Questions

Everything you need to know before submitting your case to PayResolve.

Do you guarantee payment recovery? +
No. We are committed to a professional and ethical recovery process, but cannot guarantee outcomes as recovery depends on the debtor's financial capacity and willingness. Our commitment is to give your case our very best professional effort — and you pay nothing if we don't recover.
How much do you charge? +
Our commission is typically 3%–7% of the recovered amount, agreed upfront before we start. There is absolutely NO upfront fee. You only pay after we successfully recover your money. GST @ 18% applies on the commission amount as per government regulations.
Is my information kept confidential? +
Absolutely. We sign a formal Non-Disclosure Agreement (NDA) before you share any case details. All information about you, your business, and the debtor is handled with strict confidentiality throughout the engagement and for 2 years after closure.
What documents do I need to submit a case? +
Ideally: copies of unpaid invoices, any signed agreements or purchase orders, debtor contact details, and screenshots of prior communication. Don't worry if you don't have everything — contact us first and we'll guide you on what's needed for your specific case.
How long does the recovery process take? +
Most cases are resolved within 15–45 days. The timeline depends on the debtor's responsiveness and the complexity of the case. We keep you updated at every step and set realistic expectations during the free evaluation call.
What types of cases do you handle? +
We handle B2B outstanding payment cases — unpaid invoices, outstanding dues for goods or services supplied, trade receivables, and commission dues. Minimum case value is ₹25,000. We work best with cases 30 days to 2 years old with clear supporting documents.
Do you work across all of India? +
Yes. We provide services pan India. While our base is in Gujarat, we handle cases where the debtor is in any state through professional written communication — no physical presence needed in most cases.
What if the debtor refuses to pay after your follow-up? +
If the debtor remains unresponsive or refuses to pay after our structured process, we discuss next steps with you — which may include legal notice drafting or referral to a lawyer. We'll give you an honest assessment of options and their costs.
Do you use aggressive or illegal methods? +
Absolutely not. We never use intimidation, harassment, threats, or any unlawful methods. All our communication is professional, written, and within the bounds of Indian law. This is both our ethical commitment and our legal obligation.
What if the debtor pays only partially? +
If partial recovery happens, our commission applies proportionately on the amount actually recovered. For example, if we recover ₹3 lakh out of ₹5 lakh due, commission is calculated on ₹3 lakh only.
Can I cancel the engagement midway? +
Yes. Either party can terminate the engagement with 7 days written notice. If recovery has occurred prior to termination, commission remains payable. All documents and confidential information are returned upon closure.
How do I pay your commission? +
Once you receive the recovered amount, we raise a GST-compliant commission invoice. Payment via NEFT, IMPS, or UPI within 7 working days. We provide a proper tax invoice with GSTIN for your records.
